🚨 Severe Medical Emergency?

If you or someone nearby is experiencing life-threatening symptoms, contact emergency responders immediately.

❤️ Cardiopulmonary Resuscitation (CPR)

  1. Check Safety: Verify the scene is secure for both you and the victim.
  2. Assess Responsiveness: Tap shoulders and shout "Are you okay?". Check breathing for 10 seconds.
  3. Call for Help: Shout for nearby help, dial 911, and get an AED if available.
  4. Chest Compressions: Place hands in center of chest. Push hard and fast (100–120 compressions/min, at least 2 inches deep).
  5. Rescue Breaths: If trained, give 2 rescue breaths after every 30 compressions. If untrained, perform hands-only CPR.

💨 Choking (Heimlich Maneuver)

  1. Confirm Choking: Ask "Are you choking?". Look for the universal sign (hands clutched to throat).
  2. Give Back Blows: Stand behind them, lean them forward, and deliver 5 firm blows between shoulder blades with heel of hand.
  3. Abdominal Thrusts: Wrap arms around waist. Place a fist slightly above belly button, grasp it, and press hard inward and upward 5 times.
  4. Alternate: Repeat 5 blows and 5 thrusts until object is expelled or person goes unconscious.
  5. Unconscious: If unconscious, lower to ground, call 911, and start CPR. Check mouth for obstruction.

🧠 Stroke Triage (F.A.S.T.)

  1. F - Face Drooping: Ask the person to smile. Does one side of the face droop or is it numb?
  2. A - Arm Weakness: Ask the person to raise both arms. Does one arm drift downward?
  3. S - Speech Difficulty: Ask them to repeat a simple sentence. Is their speech slurred or hard to understand?
  4. T - Time to Call 911: If they show any of these symptoms, call 911 immediately, noting the time symptoms began.

🩸 Severe Bleeding Control

  1. Apply Pressure: Place a clean cloth, bandage, or clothing directly over the wound. Press hard with both hands.
  2. Maintain Pressure: Do not release pressure to check the wound. Keep pressing continuously.
  3. Add Dressing: If blood seeps through, add more cloth on top. Do not remove the original dressing.
  4. Elevate: Elevate the bleeding limb above heart level if possible and safe to do so.
  5. Tourniquet: If bleeding is life-threatening on a limb and pressure fails, apply a tourniquet high and tight.

🔥 Thermal Burns

  1. Cool the Burn: Run cool (not cold) clean tap water over the burn for 10 to 20 minutes immediately.
  2. Remove Jewelry: Gently slide off rings, watches, or tight clothing before swelling begins.
  3. Cover Loosely: Wrap the burn with a clean, dry, non-stick sterile bandage or plastic wrap.
  4. Protect: Do not apply ice, butter, ointments, or home remedies, as they trap heat and invite infection.
  5. Seek Medical Care: Go to clinic/ER if burn is large, blistered, on face, hands, or joints.

🧪 Poisoning & Overdose

  1. Identify Poison: Determine what substance was swallowed, inhaled, or touched, and how much.
  2. Assess Status: If victim is unconscious, shaking, or struggling to breathe, call 911 immediately.
  3. Call Poison Control: If conscious, call the Poison Control Center (e.g. 1-800-222-1222 in US) for specialized advice.
  4. Do NOT Induce Vomiting: Do not give ipecac or make them throw up unless directed by a poison specialist.
  5. Flush Skin/Eyes: If on skin or eyes, rinse thoroughly with warm water for 15 minutes.
